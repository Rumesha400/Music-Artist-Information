Nessa Barrett

About:

Singer-songwriter Nessa Barrett's Warner Records debut album, young forever, features edgy, gothic pop grounded in stories about heartache, mental health, and accountability. Writing and singing have always been the saving grace that helped Barrett feel less alone in the world and less alone with her thoughts as she battled demons from her past and mental health struggles, including bipolar personality disorder and an eating disorder.

Zayn Malik

About:

Armed with just a ukulele and acoustic guitar, Punjabi singer-songwriter Anuv Jain adds natural warmth to melancholy yet reassuring tracks that have helped him become one of India's top independent artists. In 2012 at the age of 17, he wrote and uploaded a demo version of “Baarishein”, his twinkling debut single. He later rerecorded it for official release in 2018. When he was 17, he also wrote the stirring, powerful song “Maula”—written from the perspective of his twin sister—when their father sadly passed away. It was officially released in 2020. Despite Jain's songs of heartbreak, he also pens happier cuts like “Mishri” and English-language numbers such as 2018's ukulele-strummed “Ocean”.

Shawn Mendes

About:

The Local Train is a Hindi Rock Band from New Delhi that makes use of Hindi and Urdu lyrics delivering an amalgamation of ideas that they wish to express through their music. The band launched 4 singles over a year before releasing their debut album 'Aalas ka Pedh' in 2015. Their second album Vaaqif was released in January 2018 which generated very promising sales and was one of the most streamed independent albums in India.

Taylor Swift

About:

The smooth melodicism of singer/songwriter Prateek Kuhad spans graceful indie folk and tender adult pop songs largely concerned with matters of heart. Recording some of his songs in Hindi and some in English. he presented his first full-length release, In Tokens & Charms, in 2015. A deluxe edition followed two years later. In the meantime, Kuhad collected tens of millions of streams with "Kho Gaye Hum Kahan," an intimate acoustic duet with Jasleen Royal for the movie Baar Baar Dekho; enjoyed an international breakthrough with the song "cold/mess" that included a growing fan base in the U.S.; and he took home an MTV Europe music Award for Best India Act.
